Electrical Overview

Our electrical system consists primarily of 36 servos controlled by an Arduino Mega. We purchased a protoboard and soldered the servo connections to create a cleaner product, compared to using a breadboard.

  • A Look at Our Servos

    We purchased 38 Hextronik HXT900 servos from HobbyKing. Halfway through the project, we were waiting for the servos to ship, and needed some immediately, so we bought 5 Hossen SG90 servos from Amazon. These two brands of servos are very similar, being high-torque, very small motors. The Hextronik servos have slightly more torque than the Hossen servos, although the difference is negligible for their key-pressing abilities.

    We have 36 servos in our final deliverable, which span three octaves on the keyboard.

  • Using the Arduino

    To send the control signals to the motors, we use an Arduino Mega. We chose the Mega because it has both more SRAM memory and EEPROM memory, and more connections pins than the Arduino Uno we began prototyping with. The Mega sends out both the PWM control signal which changes the servo positions of each or our servos and the the power/ground.

    In order to power all 36 motors from the same power/ground we connect the wires to a protoboard, only connecting the PWM signal to each motor individually. This allows us to have cleaner wire management which is very important given the number of servos we are using in the project.
